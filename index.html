Literature Survey

This research component introduces a highly available SDN blockchain based e-voting infrastructure. Blockchain technology is often used in e-voting applications because of its characteristics such as anonymity, decentralization, immutability and verifiability. The purpose is to keep election costs as low as possible while maintaining election integrity by complying to all security, privacy, and compliance regulations. A new election technology will replace the current pen and paper method and reduce fraud while making the voting process transparent and verifiable. However, Quality of Service is a major factor during voting considering large amount of inbound/outbound traffic and overall user experience. In a blockchain based system, underlying blockchain technology’s performance plays a major role in Quality of Service of overall system. Private blockchains are often far better than public ones in terms of speed, latency, throughput, and resource usage, but they are still far below the level that enterprise requirements. Generally, every blockchain based e-voting systems consist of user registration, authentication, vote casting, vote counting and results publication. In this research, vote casting and counting phases are considered to improve QoS. User registration and authentication are not considered here as their methodologies vary according to each governments or election’s rules and regulations.

Research Problem

Voting has always been a complicated process in which a small number of influential people are entrusted with enormous amounts of authority. The way votes are counted is one of the key reasons for the high cost of existing voting systems. Various groups around the world are investigating simple solutions based on a distributed ledger, most notably the Ethereum Blockchain.

Objectives

The main Goal of the project improve the QoS in blockchain based e-voting system

  • Creating a smart contract on a blockchain powered by Ethereum.
  • Creating a Solidity smart contract with the Remix Ethereum IDE and Visual Studio Code to storing votes.
  • Making it more transparent regarding data confidentiality while maintaining its integrity for security standards and reducing the risk of fraud using Blockchain technology.
  • Create reputation algorithm to determine most suitable redundant controller to take over during master controller failure.
  • Develop smart contract algorithm for vote data insert and acquire function into Blockchain which can operate inner voting device.>

Solution

The implementation of blockchain technology within the framework of voting application development has the potential to address the challenges posed by the presence of a single point of failure. The distributed ledger technology known as blockchain makes it possible for tens of thousands of individual personal computers to collaborate and function as one, making them more powerful than a small number of centralized servers. Any centralized database is susceptible to corruption, and relying on a third party to keep the data accurate is often necessary. Blockchains, on the other hand, only attach structure and only permit information to be added to the database, which makes it difficult to change or delete blocks that have already been entered.

In place of more costly testing tools, we put our study through its paces with the help of the GNS3 network simulator. GNS3 is free, open-source software that can test and validate real-world deployments. It has been used to test and validate several deployments. GNS3 is a network engineering tool that is used on a global scale by hundreds of thousands of network engineers to simulate, set up, test, and debug virtual and real networks. You may operate tiny topologies consisting of only a few devices on a laptop with GNS3, or you can host a large number of devices on numerous servers or even in the cloud with GNS3. Software-defined networking is an approach to networking that uses software to direct traffic on the network. This model differs from traditional networking, which uses dedicated hardware devices such as routers and switches.
